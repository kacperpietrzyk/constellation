# Security policy

## Supported versions

Constellation is currently pre-alpha and has no supported release line. Security
reports are still welcome and will be handled privately.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Do not report vulnerabilities in public issues or discussions.

Use GitHub's
[private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/kacperpietrzyk/constellation/security/advisories/new)
to send the maintainers:

- the affected component or commit;
- reproduction steps or a proof of concept;
- the expected impact;
- any known mitigation;
- whether and when you intend to disclose the issue publicly.

Please avoid accessing data that is not yours, disrupting services, or
publishing the issue before a fix can be prepared. We will acknowledge the
report, assess its impact, and coordinate next steps through the private
advisory.

## Scope

Reports about dependency vulnerabilities without a demonstrated impact on
Constellation may be closed as informational. Reports involving leaked secrets,
authorization boundaries, agent permissions, imported meeting data, or audit
history are always considered relevant.
